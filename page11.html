Mount Kilimanjaro

Mount Kilimanjaro

Do you want 

to see what you're made of—to test your strength and endurance, to push yourself to the limit? Are you looking for fantastic, unique and memorable experiences? Forged from primordial flames and tempered by icy might, Mt. Kilimanjaro stands tall as a symbol of wonder and majesty. As a result of volcanic activity, Africa's tallest mountain is graced with gentle slopes which converge to three volcanic peaks:

Interesting fact

-Shira, whose plateau is to the west at an elevation of 3962 meters
-Mawenzi, the most beautiful volcano. Its western slope is adorned with cliffs and ravines. Surrounding it are deep valleys.
-Kibo, the youngest of the three volcanoes and the location of the tallest of the seven summits on Kilimanjaro—Uhuru Peak—with an elevation of 5895 meters.

You will

On your trek up the mountain you will weather a variety of harsh environments: bushland, rainforest, meadows, mountainous plains and arctic zones. Kilimanjaro, the roof of Africa, is a formidable rock giant—do you have what it takes to scale it? Don't miss your chance for a gripping and exhilarating adventure!

Pick your route

Shira Route

The route approaches from the west and practically mirrors the Lemosho route. The route begins with a gradual ascent followed by an overnight stay at Simba camp. Upon resuming, the route merges with the Lemosho route near the Shira-2 camp and then traverses the southern glacier to reach the peak from camp Barafu. The descent follows the Mweka route. This route is chosen by prepared climbers who have experience of high altitudes and who can rapidly adapt to extreme conditions. The center of the Shira plateau is a fantastic and mysterious place with stone towers and amazing views. It is here that Hemingway found some of his inspiration. From this plateau, you can easily observe elephants, buffalos, and the lush vegetation.

Ronga Route

This route is the only one approaching Kilimanjaro from the north and is located near the border with Kenya. It's a quiet route with few travelers, ideal for those wishing to avoid overcrowded climbing routes. While the views aren't as diverse as in other routes, you will be able to explore some truly untouched wilderness in the peaceful comfort of solitude. The climb is moderately difficult and is accessible to climbers still gaining experience. After scaling, you will descend down the Marangu route.

Machame Route

This climbing route approaches the peak from the southwest. It is one day longer than Marangu, but this has the advantage of easing acclimatization. The path through the rainforest leads to a wonderful view of the volcanic plateau, lava ridges, beautiful grottoes—you will see this and much more on this route. The Shira plateau offers a wonderful panoramic view.
You can climb the lava tower and the Barranco wall and traverse the Kilimanjaro glacier. The landscape is beautiful and varied. Machame is an intense walking route, but is suitable for people in excellent form who are determined to summit.

Lemosho Route

This route offers a premium trekking experience. The route starts in the moorlands, continues through a barren valley and finishes at Kilimanjaro's famous glaciers, while the descent passes through a rainforest. This is one of the more interesting climbing routes. Beginning climbers can rest at ease: the route allows for easy acclimatization while providing many beautiful sights. Lemosho is a contender for being the most pristine and scenic of Kilimanjaro's climbing routes.

Marangu Route


This is the only route with fully equipped accommodations. It is of moderate difficulty but requires lengthy acclimatization. The route approaches Kilimanjaro from the southeast side. You will have many chances to meet blue monkeys and other local fauna. The final part of the climb is along the volcanic crater's edge. As you marvel at the peak, you can also admire the great ravens.
